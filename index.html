Introduction

Nanotechnology is a multidisciplinary field that involves the manipulation and engineering of matter at the nanoscale, typically defined as dimensions ranging from 1 to 100 nanometers (nm). To put this scale into perspective, a nanometer is one-billionth of a meter, which is roughly 100,000 times smaller than the width of a human hair. At this scale, materials exhibit unique physical and chemical properties that differ significantly from their bulk counterparts, leading to innovative applications across various domains.
